I always knew I would end up being a programmer. I picked up my first computer when I was 4 and instantly fell in love with it. That, in addition to a need for creating things and knowing how they are made and work, gave shape to my passion for coding.

I tried a wide variety of languages and paths, but ultimately decided to go for the Web Development route of programming. I felt like it combined aesthetics and problem-solving in a way that just made a lot of sense for me to choose it.

I picked up HTML, CSS and JavaScript, and then dived into frameworks and libraries that just make things more and more interesting as I go. I just won't get tired of learning this stuff.

I've been developing web sites and applications since 2019, never being afraid to evolve, ask questions, recognize mistakes, and fix them.
