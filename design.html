Since I was a kid, I learn design concepts. I picked up Adobe Photoshop at the age of nine, when I would produce content for my websites and Facebook pages. I learned about the difference between bpm and png, the concept of renders and vectors and how to combine the best of both worlds to create what, at that time, seemed like a beautiful clean banner to me.

Later, when I grew up, I got my first job designing and assembling stamps. I picked up Corel Draw and used it for nearly 3 years. It was a great and valuable learning experience, but it wasn't as aestethically pleasing as I would want to.

Now, after a long run, I got into Web Design. I've made myself comfortable in Adobe XD and Figma, and know my way around Adobe Illustrator.
